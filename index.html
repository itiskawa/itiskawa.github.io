About Me

I am a Master's student in Electrical Engineering at ETH Zurich, with a strong interest in Information Theory, Quantum Communications and Applied Category Theory.
Also, I am currently a graduate assistant in the Institute for Dynamical Systems and Control.

Information and computation have always been concepts that fascinated me. Not so much on an engineering level, but more so as fundamental phenomenae in nature. Most of all, I enjoy observing and exploring my surroundings and documenting my findings. I think this is a key feature of my life. From writing songs to shooting short films, I have found myself writing down equations and schematics for research. While seemingly diametrically opposed, I believe they are reflections of creativity and observation. Emulating a musical genre, acting a specific emotion, or describing the passage of information through a channel. These are all the same to me. I have also had professional experience in Software Engineering, Digital Signal Processing, Teaching Assistant and Data Science. More on that later.

Finding Mathematics

Throughout my studies, I have found myself coming closer and closer to Mathematics.
I believe that it is due to the fact that I am fundamentally interested by nature and properties of reality, rather than learning to manipulate it for convenience. Of course, I love engineering and what it brings to the table, but I often find myself not in the same mindset of most of my engineering peers.

For that reason, I am orienting my studies towards a more 'Theoretical Engineering' or rather 'Applied Mathematics' direction. I think the latter fits better my idea of what I want to do. That said, I am focusing my studies on a few main topics, namely Information Theory and Applied Category Theory. The main idea is to satisfy my need for structure and well-behavedness from Algebra, while attempting to find it in 'chaotic' environments, such as Probability and / or Dynamical Systems. As of now, I am focusing on more theoretical approaches to both, touching up on some Tropical Geometry, Combinatorics and liks to Statistics and Machine Learning.
