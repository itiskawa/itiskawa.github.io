I am currently pursuing my Master's studies in Electrical Engineering and Information Technology at ETH Zurich. Information and computation have always been concepts that fascinated me. Not so much on an engineering level, but more so as fundamental phenomenae in nature. It is indeed a bit strange to study engineering with such a clear passion for the natural sciences and mathematics, but I am making ends meet as follows:

Finding Mathematics

Throughout my studies, I have found myself coming closer and closer to Mathematics.
I believe that it is due to the fact that I am fundamentally interested by nature and properties of reality, rather than learning to manipulate it for convenience. Of course, I love engineering and what it brings to the table, but I often find myself not in the same mindset of most of my engineering peers.

For that reason, I am orienting my studies towards a more 'Theoretical Engineering' or rather 'Applied Mathematics' direction. I think the latter fits better my idea of what I want to do. That said, I am focusing my studies on a few main topics, namely Information Theory and Applied Category Theory. The main idea is to satisfy my need for structure and well-behavedness from Algebra, while attempting to find it in 'chaotic' environments, such as Probability and / or Dynamical Systems. As of now, I am focusing on more theoretical approaches to both, touching up on some Tropical Geometry, Combinatorics and liks to Statistics and Machine Learning.
